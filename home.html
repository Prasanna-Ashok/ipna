PREAMBLE

The perioperative setting in any medical center is the vital department for surgical intervention. It runs strong on collaborative care concept. Operating room (OR) nurses are the binding factor of the team. OR nurses deal with immediate pre-operative care, intraoperative care and immediate post-operative care; hence these nurses are identified as perioperative nurses. Patient safety in the perioperative settings is of primary concern. An association for perioperative nurses will enable them to focus on educating, updating and continuing togetherness in improving perioperative patient safety across the country. Improving patient safety is an increasing priority for the surgical team and the hospitals since sentinel events can be catastrophic for patients, care givers as well as for the institutions.

VISION

The Indian Perioperative Nurses Association (IPNA) seeks to empower perioperative nurses, enable them to continue education, enhance professional development and expand the scope of perioperative nursing care in India.

OBJECTIVES

  1. To unite registered nurses across India for the purpose of maintaining an Association dedicated to promoting the highest professional standards of perioperative nursing practice for the optimal care of the patient immediately before, during, and immediately after operative procedures.
  2. To provide opportunities for continuous professional development, to include diversified educational activities.
  3. To generate, evaluate and disseminate scientific evidence to improve professional perioperative practice.
  4. To provide leadership in professional perioperative practice to influence health care delivery locally, nationally, and globally.
  5. To cooperate lawfully with other professional associations, health care facilities, universities, industries, technical societies, research organizations, and governmental agencies in matters affecting the foregoing purposes of the Association;
  6. To otherwise lawfully adopt policies and conduct programs for the improvement of professional perioperative practice provided that the policies and programs are consistent with the requirements that the Association is not organized for profit and no part of its earnings inure to individuals.

MEMBERSHIP

ALL PERIOPERATIVE NURSES are entitled to become members of the association.

  1. The members pay the membership fee, as determined by the Executive committee.
  2. The membership fee is subject to revision at the discretion of the Executive Committee from time to time.
  3. The life membership fees is Rs. 2500/- and annual membership fee Rs.250.
  4. Membership to the Association is non-transferable for any reason including death of the member.
  5. If an existing member wishes to resign from the Association, the Membership fee will not be refunded.
  6. All members are bound by the constitution of the ASSOCIATION OF PERIOPERATIVE NURSES IN INDIA (APNI) and the memorandum of the Association.
  7. Membership in the Association is contingent on compliance with requirements as specified in these bylaws.
  8. Membership is unrestricted by consideration of nationality, race, creed, lifestyle, color, gender or age.
Donor Member
  1. The Executive Committee shall be empowered to invite members to become donor members of The Association on payment Rs. 20,000/-. This amount is subject to revision by the Executive committee from time to time.
  2. The names of such donor members shall be published in the Annual Bulletin/web site of the Association.
  3. Donor members shall receive the regular publications /bulletins of the Association.
  4. These members shall also be permanent invitees to all functions of the Association.
